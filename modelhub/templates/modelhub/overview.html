GLINS Model Overview

What is GLINS?

The Global Logistics Intermordal Network Simulation (GLINS) model integrates the worldwide maritime container network with regional hinterland networks (road, rail, barge, etc.) by applying network-analysis techniques commonly used in traffic engineering. It provides end-to-end simulations of intermodal cargo flows under current or future economic and policy scenarios.

Geographic Applications

The model has already been applied to:

  • Central America (El Salvador and neighbouring countries)
  • Mekong & Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Vietnam, Myanmar)
  • South Asia (India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh)
  • Central Asia / Caucasus & Eurasia
  • Oceania, Africa, the Arctic route, and Japan

Upcoming studies will expand to East Asia (China and Korea), Eastern Europe (incl. Ukraine) and South America.

Collaboration & Policy Interface

GLINS outputs are provided to government and international agencies such as JICA, the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism of Japan, and the Asian Development Bank (ADB) for pre-feasibility studies and infrastructure-investment projects. Efficient handling of heterogeneous input data and model calibration with limited observed values are key challenges we continuously address.

Recent Extensions

  • Endogenous optimisation of carrier service networks
  • Inclusion of air-cargo networks and empty / foldable containers
  • Integrated bulk-cargo module and dry-port representation
