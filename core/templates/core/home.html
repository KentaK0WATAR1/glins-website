Mission

The GLINS (Global Logistics Intermodal Network Simulation) was developed to address the globalization of international maritime container transport and the need to comprehensively capture global flows. To support the evaluation of international logistics policies, including infrastructure development, GLINS emphasizes practical applicability over theoretical complexity, combining simplicity, reasonable accuracy, and the ability to incorporate policy variables.

It responds to the demand for prioritizing policies that span multiple countries and transport modes by integrating not only maritime networks but also hinterland networks—roads, railways, and inland waterways—into a comprehensive framework.

The model aims to reproduce the flows of international maritime container cargo on an intermodal logistics network, encompassing the global maritime transport network and the hinterland transport network of the target region.
